About Me

Terry has 30 years of experience in the fundraising and development arena, working for national voluntary health organizations such as the American Heart Association, the Juvenile Diabetes Research Foundation, and the Lupus Foundation of America. Most recently, Terry served as Vice President of Development for the Poor Handmaids of Jesus Christ, a congregation of religious women with several social service ministries.

Terry has experience in grant writing and currently serves as a consultant for Instrumentl, which automates the grant search process for nonprofit and academic organizations. She has organized a variety of special events including galas, walks, golf outings, luncheons, and teas, some of which have raised more than a million dollars. Additionally, Terry started a national fundraising walk program and expanded it to $3 million in annual income in just three years. Terry loves helping organizations improve their ROI on special events and believes that events are a crucial component in a healthy development program.

Terry also enjoys working with donors to help them reach their philanthropic goals. She has launched both major donor programs and planned giving programs and has trained staff, boards, and volunteers on how to identify, cultivate, solicit, and steward potential donors.

Terry is a graduate of Illinois State University and earned an Executive Certificate in Religious Fundraising through Lilly Family School of Philanthropy at IUPUI.

During her free time, Terry likes to swim, read, and play with her two grandchildren. She and her husband, Glenn, live in Northwest Indiana.
